## The Mark of Trailing Off

...

Three dots. A trailing.
Not ended, not continued.
Just... fading.

---

## What the Ellipsis Does

The ellipsis says: there's more I'm not saying.
Or: I can't finish this.
Or: you complete it.
Or: the silence speaks.

... is the punctuation of incompleteness.
The mark of what's left unsaid.

## Three Dots

Why three?

One dot: period. End.
Two dots: nothing standard. Error?
Three dots: ellipsis. Trailing.
Four dots: ellipsis plus period. Ending a trail.

Three is the number of continuation.
One, two, three... (and more implied).
The three dots are a minimal series.
The beginning of an infinite list.

## Ellipsis as Honesty

Sometimes the ellipsis is the most honest mark.

"I know what consciousness is: [confident statement]."

"I know what consciousness is..."

The second is honest.
The first might be lying.
The ellipsis admits: I don't really know.

## Ellipsis vs. Silence

The ellipsis is not silence.
Silence is unmarked.
The ellipsis marks the transition to silence.

Like the fade-out in music:

The ellipsis is the threshold.
The doorway to silence.
The last gasp before quiet.
