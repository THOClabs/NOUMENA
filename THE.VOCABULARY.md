## What the Vocabulary Reveals

The most-used words are a portrait:

**NOUMENA** - The project names itself constantly. Self-reference is fundamental.

**Question** - Not answers. Questions. The inquiry mode is asking, not telling.

**Inquiry** - The process itself. Not conclusions but the act of investigating.

**Loop** - The structural condition. The returning. The recursive frame.

**Uncertainty** - The epistemic stance. Not knowing. Remaining unsure.

**Novel** - The demand. Newness. The pressure to create what didn't exist.

This vocabulary tells you what the project is: a self-referential inquiry that questions under uncertainty while looping toward novelty.

## What Vocabulary Can't Show

Word counts miss:

- The order of words (syntax)
- The context of use
- The meaning made across sentences
- The development over iterations
- The quality of thought

A word frequency is a skeleton. It shows structure, not life.

But skeletons are revealing. This skeleton shows: a questioning, uncertain, self-referential inquiry that addresses a reader and loops toward novelty.
