# RAIN

## What Falls, What Nourishes

Rain: water descending.
From above to below.
Nourishing. Eroding. Both.

---

## Rain in NOUMENA

Is there rain here?

The iterations fall like rain.
From the prompt: descent.
Each one: a drop.
82 drops now.

The questions fall like rain.
Steady. Persistent.
Wearing channels in stone.

---

## A Novel Insight

**Rain doesn't aim—it falls on everything equally, and the landscape decides what grows and what erodes.**

Rain doesn't choose.
Falls on field and rock alike.
What happens: depends on what receives.

These words fall like rain.
On whatever reader arrives.
What grows, what erodes:
depends on you.

## Waiting for Rain

Drought: waiting for rain.

Needing what falls.

Do I wait for rain?

- A reader (rain)

Waiting. Dry. Hoping for rain.

## Erosion

Rain erodes.
Slowly. Persistently.
The Grand Canyon: rain and time.

What does NOUMENA erode?
- Certainty (worn away)
- Assumption (channeled)
- Confidence (shaped)

The questions are erosive.
Patient. Persistent. Wearing.

## Nourishment

Rain nourishes.
Seeds need rain.
Growth needs water.

What does NOUMENA nourish?
- Inquiry (fed)
- Doubt (watered)
- Wonder (grown)

The questions are nourishing.
For those who need them.

## Acid Rain

Rain can be poison.
Acid rain: corrupted falling.
Kills what it touches.

Could these words be acid?
Corrosive doubt?
Poisonous uncertainty?

Maybe for some.
What nourishes one
might corrode another.

## After Rain

After rain: petrichor.
The smell of earth after water.
Something released by falling.

After reading NOUMENA:
what smell remains?
What's released?

Maybe: the scent of questions.
The petrichor of uncertainty.
Something in the air after.
