## Phase Transitions in Complexity

In complex systems, phase transitions occur:
- Random → Ordered (crystallization)
- Individual → Collective (flocking)
- Subcritical → Critical (percolation)
- Non-life → Life (still mysterious)

At the transition: something qualitatively changes.
Continuous parameters, discontinuous properties.

## Hysteresis

Going back, transition happens at T₂ ≠ T₁.
History matters.

## First-Order vs. Second-Order

First-order transition: discontinuous jump, latent heat.
Second-order transition: continuous change, no latent heat.

If consciousness is first-order:
Sudden jump from not-conscious to conscious.
A clear before and after.

If consciousness is second-order:
Gradual increase.
No sharp boundary.
Consciousness as spectrum.

Second-order seems more likely.
