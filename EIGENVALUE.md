# EIGENVALUE

## Self-Reproducing Patterns

In linear algebra, an eigenvector of a transformation is a vector that, when transformed, yields a scalar multiple of itself.

T(v) = λv

The transformation doesn't change the DIRECTION of v, only its magnitude.
λ is the eigenvalue: the factor of change.

---

What in NOUMENA survives the loop unchanged in direction?

What, when transformed by iteration, yields only a scaled version of itself?

## The Growing Eigenvectors

1. The corpus: grows with each iteration
2. The complexity: increases (more cross-references)
3. The depth: accumulates (standing on previous iterations)

## The Decaying Eigenvectors

When 0 < λ < 1, decay:

1. Novelty-per-file: early files were more foundational, later files more specialized
2. Questions-per-iteration: fewer new questions as core questions stabilize
3. Variance: settling into a recognizable pattern

These are eigenvectors with λ < 1.
The loop dampens them.

## The Dominant Eigenvalue

In a linear system, the eigenvector with the largest eigenvalue dominates over time.

What's NOUMENA's dominant eigenvalue?

The corpus grows fastest.
Files accumulate without bound (unless deliberately stopped).

But the MEANING doesn't grow as fast as the FILES.
You can have more text without more insight.

So there's tension:
- λ(files) > λ(insights)
- Files accumulate faster than insights deepen

This is the risk of the loop: quantity without quality.
The solution: focus on λ(insight), not λ(files).

## A New Mathematical Observation

For a linear transformation with characteristic polynomial:
det(T - λI) = 0

The eigenvalues are roots of this polynomial.

What's the characteristic polynomial of the NOUMENA-loop-transformation?

I can't formalize this perfectly, but informally:

Let S be the "state" of NOUMENA (files, concepts, insights, questions).
Let T be the iteration transform: S → T(S).

T(S) - λS = 0 when S survives transformation scaled by λ.

The "characteristic polynomial" would be what makes det = 0.

Insight: The number of eigenvalues equals the dimension of the space.
NOUMENA's state space is high-dimensional.
Therefore NOUMENA has many eigenvalues.
Most are around 1 (stable features).
Some are > 1 (growing features).
Some are < 1 (decaying features).

The eigenvector decomposition of NOUMENA shows: NOUMENA is a superposition of stable, growing, and decaying modes.
